The mental health crisis is real.
I know it because I see it everyday at my college campus. Not a semester goes by where we don’t get an email about a fellow student’s suicide. It prompts us to check-in on our friends again, but this is not the kind of motivation we need.
Overview
I was contacted by a few grad students from Stanford Unversity who noticed the same issue at their campus and were actually trying to solve it (or at least improve the situation). We were a team of three designers who worked under the guidance and feedback from Yingzhao Liu (LinkedIn) and Jamal Nichols (Facebook). We were helping young adults master the habit of showing up for their own wellbeing. My role was to work on information architecture, visual and interaction design. I also worked closely with the engineers to ensure that the designs were implemented successfully.
Problem Statement
How might we empower healthy and resilient individuals and communities by reinforcing the inextricable link between self-care and caring
for others.
